Air Assault Division

101st Airborne Division

A light infantry division of the United States Army that specializes in air assault operations — planning, coordinating, and executing missions to seize terrain across large distances, behind enemy lines, and in austere environments with limited infrastructure.

"Rendezvous with Destiny!"

Order of Battle

Three Brigades, One Division

Its unique battlefield mobility and high level of training have kept the 101st in the vanguard of U.S. land combat forces. Every soldier belongs to one of three brigades, each with its own role and standard.

Black Hats Brigade

Composed strictly of instructors, the Black Hats are the backbone of the division — responsible for mass recruiting and running Air Assault School, training up recruits and giving them the instruction needed to prepare for the division. They hold one of the highest standards among personnel.

"Do It Right."

Wings Brigade

Officially the 101st Combat Aviation Brigade, Wings executes air assault operations and provides aerial support for ground forces. Pilots graduate from education programs in Kronos and Blizzard, then specialize across Air Logistics, Air Assault, and Pilot Qualification.

"Wings of Destiny!"

Strike Brigade

The main fighting brigade of the division, Strike fields cavalrymen, infantrymen, drivers, reconnaissance elements, and combat medics. As the largest brigade, they represent the standard soldier — personnel graduating Air Assault School are placed here to meet the division's high standards.

"First to Strike!"
